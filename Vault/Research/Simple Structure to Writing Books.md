# Writing Structure for Obsidian

Simple structure. The goal is to write without losing control of the project.

---

# Structure

```text  
(Book Name)  
├── Dashboard  
├── Chapters  
├── Scenes  
├── Assets  
│   ├── Brainstorm  
│   ├── Ideas  
│   ├── Characters  
│   ├── World  
│   ├── Timeline  
│   └── Research  
├── Revision  
└── Archive  
```

---

**# Folder Roles**

**## Dashboard**

Project center.

Keep:

- progress
- main links
- manuscript problems
- current focus

Example:

```md  
# Book Name

## Current Focus  
[[Scene 21]]

## Problems  
- Slow middle  
- Weak antagonist

## Links  
- [[Outline]]  
- [[Timeline]]  
- [[Character Arcs]]  
```

---

## Chapters

Editorial structure.

Chapters are not the writing itself. They are organization.

Example:

```text  
Chapters  
├── Chapter 01  
├── Chapter 02  
└── Chapter 03  
```

Can be:

- simple files
- scene indexes
- short structural notes

Used to control narrative order.

---

## Scenes

The actual writing layer.

Each file:

- one scene
- one conflict
- one narrative unit

Example:

```text  
Scenes  
├── Arrival.md  
├── The Letter.md  
└── Interrogation.md  
```

Scene separation makes it easier to:

- move material
- cut scenes
- revise pacing
- restructure chapters

A strong scene should survive outside the chapter.

---

## Assets

Support material.

Not manuscript.

---

### Brainstorm

Raw input.

Everything goes here:

- dialogue
- phrases
- ideas
- mental images
- vague scenes

No over-organization.

---

### Ideas

Concepts that survived Brainstorm.

More defined:

- conflicts
- arcs
- concepts
- relationships

---

### Characters

One file per relevant character.

Keep:

- desire
- fear
- contradiction
- arc
- voice

Do not turn characters into RPG sheets.

---

### World

Everything tied to the setting:

- locations
- factions
- systems
- culture
- politics

Only document what affects the story.

---

### Timeline

Project chronology.

Important for:

- continuity
- pacing
- causality

Example:

```md  
Day 1  
- Arrival  
- Riot

Day 2  
- Murder  
- Escape  
```

---

### Research

External material.

Examples:

- PDFs
- references
- images
- studies
- technical notes

Never mix research with manuscript.

---

## Revision

Separate from drafting.

Keep:

- problems
- inconsistencies
- cuts
- feedback
- structural revisions

Drafting and revision should not occupy the same mental space.

---

## Archive

Dead material.

Examples:

- cut scenes
- old drafts
- abandoned ideas
- paused projects

Do not delete everything.

Just remove it from the active flow.

---

# Metadata

Use little.

```yaml  
status:  
arc:  
pov:  
```

Example:

```yaml  
status: draft  
arc: betrayal  
pov: Helena  
```

Enough to filter scenes without turning the vault into a database.

# Rules

- Manuscript is narrative only.
- Assets do not replace writing.
- Worldbuilding exists to support story.
- Organization should reduce friction, not create work.
- If the system takes more time than the manuscript, the system failed.
